Degree of Success

The Resolution Matrix

DegreeResultTypeEffect
25+Extraordinary SuccessExceptionalCinematic outcome. Extra benefits at GM’s discretion.
20–24Major SuccessExceptionalOutstanding success with significant advantage.
15–19SuccessStandardTask accomplished as intended.
10–14Partial SuccessStandardSuccess with a cost, complication, or limitation.
5–9Minor SuccessMarginalBare success. Minimal progress or fragile outcome.
1–4FailureFailureTask fails. GM introduces a consequence.
0 or lessCritical FailureFailureSevere consequence. Situation worsens.

Degree of Success is compared to the Target Number (TN) set by the GM.
Higher totals yield better outcomes. Modifiers apply before comparison.

Checks & Target Numbers

A Check is made when the outcome is uncertain and meaningful.

  • Easy Tasks: TN 5–9
  • Average Tasks: TN 10–14
  • Difficult Tasks: TN 15–19
  • Extreme Tasks: TN 20+

The GM sets the TN based on complexity, opposition, environment, and narrative weight.

Opposed Tasks

When two or more characters actively oppose one another, each makes a Check.

Highest Degree of Success wins.

Ties go to the defender.

Helping

A character may assist another who is attempting a task. The helper makes a Check.

Success grants a +2 bonus to the primary character.

Turns & Actions

Each character, per turn:

  • Major Actions — 1
  • Minor Actions — 1
  • Swift Actions — 1
  • Reactions — Conditional

Plan carefully. Every action matters.

Natural 20 / Natural 1

Natural 20: Automatic success. Apply Degree of Success as normal.

Natural 1: Automatic failure. Apply Degree of Success as normal.

These are not Critical Hits or Fumbles.

Try, Try Again

A character may repeat a failed Check during the same scene.

Each retry increases the TN by +5.

Narrate the cost of pushing fate further.

Adjusting Complexity

The Engine bends to the Tale.

Simplify for speed.

Add depth for drama.

You decide how complex or cinematic your game will be.

The Engine is a Framework, Not a Prison

The Eternity Engine is designed to be adaptable. Use these rules as written, or modify them to fit your Table, your Players, and your Story.

What matters is not the rules themselves, but the legends you forge together.
